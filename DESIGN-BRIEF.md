# 4D Supper Club — Design Brief

## Brand Positioning

"Private chef meets Apple."

This is NOT a meal kit. NOT grocery delivery. NOT restaurant takeout.
This is a curated weekly food membership.

Users should feel: "I belong here." "I trust the quality." "This company has its act together."

Exclusive without feeling elitist.

## Primary Design Objective

Within 3 seconds every screen answers:
1. What meal is next?
2. When does ordering close?  
3. What should I do next?

## Visual Direction

Food is the hero. Large editorial photography, tight food crops, natural lighting, premium presentation, magazine-quality imagery.

Avoid: stock-food vibes, cluttered overlays, tiny thumbnails, generic ecommerce grids.

Think: luxury cookbook, Michelin restaurant, private chef service, premium hospitality brand.

## Design Qualities

Every screen communicates: Curated, Effortless, Modern, Premium, Intentional, Operationally excellent.

Never: flashy, startup gradient soup, Dribbble-inspired, generic SaaS.

## Premium Restraint

Reduce: borders, visual noise, excessive shadows, excessive colors, decorative elements.
Increase: white space, alignment precision, typography hierarchy, content quality, breathing room.

The product should feel expensive because of what is missing.

## Reference Apps

Apple Sports, Uber, Airbnb, Linear.

## Core Innovation: Weekly Ritual

The app revolves around a weekly cycle:
- MONDAY: Menu Released
- TUESDAY: Most Popular Meals Trending
- WEDNESDAY: Order Closes In 12 Hours
- THURSDAY: Chef Preparation Begins
- FRIDAY: Packaging & Logistics
- SUNDAY: Delivery Day

## Home Screen: Command Center (not dashboard)

Top: Weekly Status Capsule — always visible signature element.
Examples: "Chef Selection Active", "3 Meals Scheduled", "Delivery Sunday", "Order Closes In 8 Hours"

## Smart Trust System

No traditional ratings. Use confidence signals:
"127 members receiving this week", "Most reordered meal", "Chef recommended", "Seasonal favorite", "Limited availability"

## Ordering Language

Membership-driven, not transaction-driven:
- "Select For This Week" not "Add To Cart"
- "Reserve Meal" not "Buy"
- "Chef Choice" not "Best Seller"

## Empty States

Never "No meals available." Instead: "Next menu arrives Monday at 9:00 AM", "Chef selections are being prepared."

## Typography

Typography carries the design. Large confident headlines, clear hierarchy, generous spacing, limited font weights. Should feel premium with all colors removed.

## Motion

Smooth, calm, deliberate, physics-based, nearly invisible. Shared element transitions, subtle elevation changes, soft fades. No bouncy cards, no gimmicky interactions.

## Quality Test

For every screen: Would a member proudly screenshot this and send it to a friend?

The emotional reaction: "This feels different." "This feels premium." "This feels like a membership I want to belong to."

Design for timelessness. Something that feels elite five years from now.
